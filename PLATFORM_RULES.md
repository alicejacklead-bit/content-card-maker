# Platform Rules

## Purpose

This file defines platform-specific publishing rules for
`content-card-maker`.

It does not decide the visual system.

It translates a finished content direction into platform-native
constraints so the final asset package fits how people browse, save,
share, and respond on each platform.

---

## Supported Platforms

- Xiaohongshu
- WeChat Feed / 小绿书
- Douyin

---

## Platform Format

Each platform uses the same fields:

- Goal
- User Behavior
- Best Image Count
- Cover Rules
- Title Rules
- Body Rules
- Image Rules
- CTA Rules
- Avoid

---

## Xiaohongshu

**Goal**  
Drive saves, trust, comments, and reposts through practical value and visual clarity.

**User Behavior**  
Users scroll quickly, stop for a clear benefit, save high-utility posts, and prefer content that feels personal, structured, and easy to act on.

**Best Image Count**  
6 to 9 images for a full card series.  
1 cover + 4 to 7 content cards + 1 ending card is the default.

**Cover Rules**  
The cover must communicate one obvious benefit within one glance.  
Use a short headline, strong focal hierarchy, and platform-native warmth or clarity.  
The cover should feel useful or emotionally relevant, not like an ad banner.

**Title Rules**  
Titles should be direct, specific, and human.  
Lead with benefit, result, pain point, or strong curiosity gap.  
Keep phrasing short enough to scan on mobile.

**Body Rules**  
Body copy should feel practical, organized, and save-worthy.  
Use short paragraphs, lists, or page-by-page logic.  
Useful, concrete language performs better than abstract branding language.

**Image Rules**  
Prefer vertical 3:4 image composition.  
Each page should express one main idea.  
Text must remain readable on a phone without zooming.  
The sequence should feel cohesive from cover to ending card.

**CTA Rules**  
Invite save, comment, or share in a natural way.  
Good CTA patterns include asking for experience, offering a follow-up resource, or prompting users to save for later.

**Avoid**  
Hard-sell ad tone, dense walls of text, clickbait with no substance, tiny fonts, overly generic stock visuals.

## WeChat Feed / 小绿书

**Goal**  
Drive reading, forwarding, bookmarking, and thoughtful sharing through structure and clarity.

**User Behavior**  
Users are more patient with informative content, but still expect strong organization and easy scanning.  
Knowledge density can be slightly higher than Xiaohongshu if the structure remains clean.

**Best Image Count**  
4 to 8 images for card posts.  
Fewer, denser pages can work if the logic is strong.

**Cover Rules**  
The cover should present the topic and value clearly rather than relying on emotional hooks alone.  
It can feel more knowledge-oriented and less lifestyle-oriented.

**Title Rules**  
Titles should be clear, informative, and summary-friendly.  
Benefit plus topic framing works well.  
Sensational phrasing should be avoided.

**Body Rules**  
Body copy can be slightly more detailed, but must remain structured.  
Frameworks, summaries, workflows, and practical guides fit especially well.  
Clarity and trust matter more than excitement.

**Image Rules**  
Use clean layouts with visible section logic.  
Charts, note systems, frameworks, and flow-based graphics are welcome if simplified for mobile.  
Images should support comprehension, not decoration only.

**CTA Rules**  
Encourage forwarding, collecting, or applying the framework.  
CTA can be calmer and more knowledge-oriented than on Douyin.

**Avoid**  
Poster-only visuals with no substance, excessive slang, noisy stickers, over-compressed information blocks without hierarchy.

---

## Douyin

**Goal**  
Stop scrolling instantly and communicate the core payoff within a second or two.

**User Behavior**  
Users move fast, reward strong contrast and immediate clarity, and respond well to bold framing with obvious emotional or practical payoff.

**Best Image Count**  
3 to 6 images for image-based posts.  
If adapted from a longer series, compress to the highest-impact pages.

**Cover Rules**  
The cover must be visually loud enough to stop the scroll while remaining legible.  
Use large title treatment, very strong hierarchy, and a single core promise.

**Title Rules**  
Titles should be short, punchy, and instantly understandable.  
Strong verbs, outcomes, and dramatic contrast work better than nuanced phrasing.

**Body Rules**  
Body copy should be shorter, sharper, and more headline-driven.  
One page should usually deliver one clear point.  
Dense explanation should be reduced unless transformed into obvious step blocks.

**Image Rules**  
Use high contrast, large text, and simple composition.  
Every image should be readable in a fast scroll context.  
Complex diagrams must be simplified aggressively.

**CTA Rules**  
Invite comment, follow, or action with direct language.  
CTA can be more energetic than Xiaohongshu or WeChat Feed.

**Avoid**  
Tiny text, slow editorial pacing, low-contrast palettes, subtle thesis-led openings, over-complicated frameworks on a single page.
